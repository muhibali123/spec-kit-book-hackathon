## Key Concepts

- **Service Robotics**: Robots designed to perform useful tasks for humans in service environments
- **Customer Experience Design**: Creating positive interactions between robots and customers
- **Social Navigation**: Moving through crowds and social spaces appropriately
- **Conversational AI**: Natural language processing for customer interactions
- **Cultural Adaptation**: Adjusting robot behavior for different cultural contexts
- **Service Quality Metrics**: Measuring effectiveness of robotic service delivery
- **Human-Robot Collaboration**: Coordinating with human staff in service environments
- **Customer Acceptance**: Factors influencing customer comfort with service robots

## Theory Summary

Service robotics represents a rapidly growing application area for humanoid robots, with deployments in hospitality, retail, healthcare, and other customer-facing industries. These robots must excel at human interaction while performing specific service tasks, requiring a unique combination of social skills, task execution capabilities, and environmental awareness.

The primary challenge in service robotics is balancing automation with the human touch that customers often expect and value. Service robots must be able to handle the unpredictable nature of customer interactions while maintaining the efficiency and consistency that automation provides. This requires sophisticated perception, natural language processing, and adaptive behavior systems.

Customer experience design for service robots involves creating interactions that feel natural and helpful rather than mechanical or intrusive. This includes appropriate timing of interactions, suitable response patterns, and the ability to recognize and respond to customer emotional states. The robot's physical appearance and movement patterns also significantly impact customer perception and acceptance.

Social navigation in service environments is particularly challenging because robots must move through spaces populated by people who may behave unpredictably. The robot must follow social conventions for movement while avoiding collisions and maintaining customer comfort. This includes understanding personal space, appropriate eye contact, and social signaling.

Conversational AI systems for service robots must handle a wide range of customer queries and requests, often with limited domain knowledge. The system must be able to gracefully handle requests outside its capabilities while maintaining a helpful and friendly demeanor. Multi-modal interaction combining speech, gestures, and visual displays often provides the best user experience.

Cultural adaptation is crucial for service robots operating in international markets. Different cultures have varying expectations about appropriate robot behavior, personal space, eye contact, and interaction styles. A successful service robot must be able to adapt its behavior to match local cultural norms.

Service quality metrics for robots differ from traditional service metrics because they must account for both task completion and customer satisfaction. Key metrics include task success rate, customer satisfaction scores, interaction time, and the robot's ability to handle edge cases without human intervention.

Human-robot collaboration in service environments requires careful coordination between automated systems and human staff. Robots should enhance rather than replace human workers, taking on routine tasks while allowing humans to focus on complex customer needs and relationship building.

Customer acceptance of service robots varies significantly based on factors such as age, cultural background, previous technology exposure, and the specific service context. Understanding these factors is crucial for successful deployment.

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from typing import Dict, List, Tuple, Optional, Any
import random
import json
from dataclasses import dataclass, asdict
from enum import Enum
from datetime import datetime, timedelta
import re

class CustomerMood(Enum):
    """Customer mood states"""
    HAPPY = "happy"
    NEUTRAL = "neutral"
    CONFUSED = "confused"
    ANNOYED = "annoyed"
    EXCITED = "excited"

class ServiceTask(Enum):
    """Types of service tasks"""
    GREETING = "greeting"
    TAKING_ORDER = "taking_order"
    ANSWERING_QUESTIONS = "answering_questions"
    GUIDING = "guiding"
    PROCESSING_PAYMENT = "processing_payment"
    CLEANING = "cleaning"
    ASSISTANCE = "assistance"

class InteractionMode(Enum):
    """Modes of interaction"""
    AUTONOMOUS = "autonomous"
    HUMAN_ASSISTED = "human_assisted"
    ESCALATION = "escalation"

@dataclass
class Customer:
    """Represents a customer in the service environment"""
    id: str
    name: str
    mood: CustomerMood
    wait_time: int  # Minutes waited
    order: Optional[Dict] = None
    satisfaction: float = 5.0  # 1-10 scale
    language: str = "English"
    cultural_preference: str = "standard"

@dataclass
class MenuItem:
    """Represents a menu item"""
    id: str
    name: str
    description: str
    price: float
    category: str
    dietary_info: List[str]  # e.g., ["vegetarian", "gluten-free"]

@dataclass
class Order:
    """Represents a customer order"""
    customer_id: str
    items: List[Dict]  # [{"item_id": str, "quantity": int, "special_requests": str}]
    status: str  # "pending", "preparing", "ready", "delivered"
    timestamp: datetime

class ServiceRobot:
    """Service robot for customer interaction in service environments"""

    def __init__(self, name: str = "ServeBot", location: str = "Restaurant"):
        self.name = name
        self.location = location
        self.current_customers: Dict[str, Customer] = {}
        self.menu: List[MenuItem] = self._initialize_menu()
        self.orders: List[Order] = []
        self.interaction_history: List[Dict] = []
        self.current_time = datetime.now()
        self.battery_level = 100.0
        self.location_coords = (0, 0)  # Current position in the service area
        self.interaction_mode = InteractionMode.AUTONOMOUS
        self.service_metrics = {
            'interactions': 0,
            'satisfaction_score': 0.0,
            'task_completion_rate': 0.0,
            'human_esc_count': 0
        }

    def _initialize_menu(self) -> List[MenuItem]:
        """Initialize the restaurant menu"""
        return [
            MenuItem("B001", "Classic Burger", "Beef patty with lettuce, tomato, and special sauce", 12.99, "Main Course", ["contains meat"]),
            MenuItem("S001", "Caesar Salad", "Romaine lettuce with parmesan, croutons, and caesar dressing", 9.99, "Salad", ["vegetarian"]),
            MenuItem("P001", "Margherita Pizza", "Tomato sauce, mozzarella, and fresh basil", 14.99, "Main Course", ["vegetarian"]),
            MenuItem("D001", "Chocolate Cake", "Rich chocolate cake with vanilla ice cream", 7.99, "Dessert", ["vegetarian"]),
            MenuItem("B002", "Veggie Burger", "Plant-based patty with vegetables and special sauce", 13.99, "Main Course", ["vegetarian", "vegan"]),
            MenuItem("S002", "Greek Salad", "Mixed greens with feta, olives, and greek dressing", 10.99, "Salad", ["vegetarian"]),
            MenuItem("D002", "Fruit Parfait", "Layered yogurt with fresh berries", 6.99, "Dessert", ["vegetarian", "vegan"])
        ]

    def detect_customer(self, customer_id: str, name: str = "Guest") -> Customer:
        """Detect and register a new customer"""
        if customer_id not in self.current_customers:
            # Determine initial mood based on arrival time and other factors
            mood = random.choice(list(CustomerMood))

            customer = Customer(
                id=customer_id,
                name=name,
                mood=mood,
                wait_time=0
            )
            self.current_customers[customer_id] = customer

            # Generate greeting based on mood
            greeting = self._generate_greeting(customer)
            self._log_interaction(customer_id, ServiceTask.GREETING, greeting)

            return customer
        else:
            return self.current_customers[customer_id]

    def _generate_greeting(self, customer: Customer) -> str:
        """Generate appropriate greeting based on customer mood and preferences"""
        greetings = {
            CustomerMood.HAPPY: f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}. You look like you're having a great day!",
            CustomerMood.NEUTRAL: f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}. How can I assist you today?",
            CustomerMood.CONFUSED: f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}. Don't worry, I'm here to help you.",
            CustomerMood.ANNOYED: f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}. I hope I can make your experience pleasant.",
            CustomerMood.EXCITED: f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}. Your enthusiasm is wonderful!"
        }

        return greetings.get(customer.mood, f"Hello {customer.name}! Welcome to {self.location}.")

    def handle_customer_request(self, customer_id: str, request: str) -> str:
        """Handle various customer requests"""
        if customer_id not in self.current_customers:
            return "I don't see you in my customer list. Let me find you first."

        customer = self.current_customers[customer_id]

        # Process the request using simple pattern matching
        request_lower = request.lower()

        if any(word in request_lower for word in ["menu", "what", "offer", "food", "eat"]):
            return self._show_menu(customer)

        elif any(word in request_lower for word in ["order", "buy", "get", "food"]):
            return self._start_order_process(customer)

        elif any(word in request_lower for word in ["table", "seat", "sit", "place"]):
            return self._guide_to_table(customer)

        elif any(word in request_lower for word in ["question", "ask", "help"]):
            return self._answer_questions(customer, request)

        elif any(word in request_lower for word in ["bathroom", "restroom", "toilet", "washroom"]):
            return "The restrooms are located straight down the hall, to your left."

        elif any(word in request_lower for word in ["bill", "check", "payment", "pay"]):
            return self._process_payment(customer)

        else:
            # If uncertain, ask for clarification or offer help
            return f"I'm not sure I understood, {customer.name}. Would you like to see our menu, place an order, or do you have a specific question?"

    def _show_menu(self, customer: Customer) -> str:
        """Show menu items to customer"""
        menu_categories = {}
        for item in self.menu:
            if item.category not in menu_categories:
                menu_categories[item.category] = []
            menu_categories[item.category].append(f"{item.name} - ${item.price:.2f}")

        menu_text = f"Here's our menu, {customer.name}:\n"
        for category, items in menu_categories.items():
            menu_text += f"\n{category}:\n"
            for item in items:
                menu_text += f"  â€¢ {item}\n"

        menu_text += "\nWould you like to place an order or do you have questions about any items?"
        return menu_text

    def _start_order_process(self, customer: Customer) -> str:
        """Start the order taking process"""
        return f"Great, {customer.name}! I can help you place an order. Please let me know what you'd like to order. You can say something like 'I'd like the Classic Burger' or 'One Caesar Salad please.'"

    def _guide_to_table(self, customer: Customer) -> str:
        """Guide customer to available table"""
        # In a real system, this would integrate with a table management system
        table_number = random.randint(1, 20)
        return f"{customer.name}, I can guide you to table {table_number}. Please follow me, and I'll lead you to your seat."

    def _answer_questions(self, customer: Customer, question: str) -> str:
        """Answer customer questions about menu items or services"""
        # Simple question answering based on keywords
        if any(word in question.lower() for word in ["vegetarian", "veggie", "meatless", "plant"]):
            veg_items = [item.name for item in self.menu if "vegetarian" in item.dietary_info]
            if veg_items:
                return f"We have several vegetarian options: {', '.join(veg_items)}. Would you like more details about any of these?"
            else:
                return "We don't have specific vegetarian options, but I can check with the kitchen about modifications."

        elif any(word in question.lower() for word in ["spicy", "hot", "pepper", "chili"]):
            return "Our kitchen can prepare dishes with varying spice levels. Please let me know your preference when ordering."

        elif any(word in question.lower() for word in ["allergy", "dietary", "restriction", "gluten", "nuts"]):
            return "We take dietary restrictions seriously. Please inform me of any allergies when placing your order, and I'll ensure the kitchen is aware."

        else:
            return f"I'd be happy to answer your question, {customer.name}. Our menu items are prepared fresh daily, and we can accommodate most special requests. Is there something specific you'd like to know?"

    def _process_payment(self, customer: Customer) -> str:
        """Process payment for customer"""
        # Check if customer has an order
        customer_orders = [order for order in self.orders if order.customer_id == customer.id]

        if not customer_orders:
            return f"You don't have any active orders, {customer.name}. Would you like to place an order first?"

        # Find pending or ready orders
        active_orders = [order for order in customer_orders if order.status in ["ready", "delivered"]]

        if not active_orders:
            return f"Your order is still being prepared, {customer.name}. Would you like me to check on its status?"

        # Calculate total
        total = 0
        for order in active_orders:
            for item in order.items:
                menu_item = next((mi for mi in self.menu if mi.id == item['item_id']), None)
                if menu_item:
                    total += menu_item.price * item['quantity']

        return f"Your total is ${total:.2f}, {customer.name}. You can pay at the front desk or I can guide you there. Payment options include credit card, cash, or mobile payment."

    def take_order(self, customer_id: str, order_items: List[Dict]) -> str:
        """Take and process customer order"""
        if customer_id not in self.current_customers:
            return "I don't see you in my customer list. Let me find you first."

        customer = self.current_customers[customer_id]

        # Validate order items
        valid_items = []
        invalid_items = []

        for item in order_items:
            menu_item = next((mi for mi in self.menu if mi.id == item['item_id']), None)
            if menu_item:
                valid_items.append(item)
            else:
                invalid_items.append(item['item_id'])

        if invalid_items:
            return f"I couldn't find the following items: {', '.join(invalid_items)}. Here are the valid options: {[item.name for item in self.menu]}"

        # Create order
        order = Order(
            customer_id=customer_id,
            items=valid_items,
            status="pending",
            timestamp=datetime.now()
        )

        self.orders.append(order)

        # Prepare confirmation message
        item_names = []
        for item in valid_items:
            menu_item = next(mi for mi in self.menu if mi.id == item['item_id'])
            item_names.append(f"{item['quantity']}x {menu_item.name}")

        order_summary = ", ".join(item_names)
        preparation_time = random.randint(15, 45)  # 15-45 minutes

        confirmation = f"Thank you, {customer.name}! Your order has been placed: {order_summary}. Estimated preparation time is {preparation_time} minutes. I'll update you when it's ready."

        self._log_interaction(customer_id, ServiceTask.TAKING_ORDER, confirmation)
        return confirmation

    def update_order_status(self, order_id: int, new_status: str) -> bool:
        """Update the status of an order"""
        if 0 <= order_id < len(self.orders):
            self.orders[order_id].status = new_status
            return True
        return False

    def _log_interaction(self, customer_id: str, task: ServiceTask, content: str):
        """Log customer interaction"""
        interaction = {
            'timestamp': datetime.now().isoformat(),
            'customer_id': customer_id,
            'task': task.value,
            'content': content,
            'location': self.location
        }
        self.interaction_history.append(interaction)
        self.service_metrics['interactions'] += 1

    def get_service_metrics(self) -> Dict:
        """Get service performance metrics"""
        total_interactions = len(self.interaction_history)
        if total_interactions > 0:
            avg_satisfaction = sum(c.satisfaction for c in self.current_customers.values()) / len(self.current_customers) if self.current_customers else 5.0
        else:
            avg_satisfaction = 5.0

        return {
            'total_customers_served': len(self.current_customers),
            'total_interactions': total_interactions,
            'average_satisfaction': avg_satisfaction,
            'active_orders': len([o for o in self.orders if o.status in ["pending", "preparing"]]),
            'completed_orders': len([o for o in self.orders if o.status == "delivered"])
        }

def simulate_service_robot():
    """Simulate a service robot in a restaurant environment"""
    print("Service Robot Simulation")
    print("=" * 30)

    # Create service robot
    robot = ServiceRobot(name="ServeBot-01", location="TechBurger Restaurant")

    # Simulate customer interactions

    # Customer requests menu

    print(f"Robot: {response}")

        {"item_id": "D001", "quantity": 1, "special_requests": "extra whipped cream"}
    ]
    order_response = robot.take_order("CUST001", order_items)
    print(f"Robot: {order_response}")

    print("\nCustomer 2 enters - Bob")
    customer2 = robot.detect_customer("CUST002", "Bob")

    # Customer asks about vegetarian options
    print(f"\nBob: Are there any vegetarian options?")
    response = robot.handle_customer_request("CUST002", "Are there any vegetarian options?")

    # Bob orders vegetarian items
    print(f"\nBob: I'll have the Veggie Burger and Greek Salad")
    order_items_bob = [
        {"item_id": "B002", "quantity": 1, "special_requests": "add avocado"},
        {"item_id": "S002", "quantity": 1, "special_requests": "dressing on the side"}
    ]
    order_response_bob = robot.take_order("CUST002", order_items_bob)
    print(f"Robot: {order_response_bob}")

    # Customer asks for directions
    print(f"\nBob: Where are the restrooms?")
    restroom_response = robot.handle_customer_request("CUST002", "Where are the restrooms?")
    print(f"Robot: {restroom_response}")

    # Simulate order completion
    print(f"\nSimulating order preparation...")
    robot.update_order_status(0, "ready")  # Alice's order
    robot.update_order_status(1, "ready")  # Bob's order

    print(f"\nRobot: {customer1.name}, your order is ready! Please proceed to the pickup counter.")
    print(f"Robot: {customer2.name}, your order is ready! Please proceed to the pickup counter.")

    # Get service metrics
    print(f"\nService Metrics:")
    metrics = robot.get_service_metrics()
    for key, value in metrics.items():

    # Update customer satisfaction
    robot.current_customers["CUST001"].satisfaction = 8.5
    robot.current_customers["CUST002"].satisfaction = 9.0

    print(f"\nUpdated satisfaction scores:")
    for customer_id, customer in robot.current_customers.items():
        print(f"  {customer.name}: {customer.satisfaction}/10")

    print(f"\nTotal interactions logged: {len(robot.interaction_history)}")
    print("Service robot simulation completed!")
