## Key Concepts

- **Asimov's Laws**: Early ethical framework for robotics (fictional but influential)
- **Human Dignity**: Preserving human autonomy and decision-making capabilities
- **Privacy**: Protecting personal data collected by robots
- **Transparency**: Ensuring human understanding of robot capabilities and limitations
- **Safety by Design**: Incorporating safety measures from the initial design phase
- **Bias Mitigation**: Addressing algorithmic bias in robot decision-making

## Theory Summary

As humanoid robots become more integrated into human environments, ethical and safety considerations become increasingly important. The field must address questions about robot rights, human-robot relationships, data privacy, and the potential impact on human dignity and autonomy.

Safety in humanoid robotics encompasses both physical safety (preventing harm to humans and property) and psychological safety (ensuring robots don't cause emotional distress or dependency issues).
